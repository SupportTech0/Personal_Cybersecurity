Step-by-Step Guide: What to Do If Your Email Is Hacked

If your email account gets hacked, it's critical to act quickly to minimize damage and regain control. Here's a step-by-step guide:

1. Secure Your Device

  • Run a full antivirus scan to ensure your device isn't compromised by malware or keyloggers.
  • Update your device's operating system and all applications.

2. Regain Access to Your Email Account

  • Try logging into your email account immediately.
  • If you can't log in, use the "Forgot Password" option to reset it.
  • Follow the account recovery process provided by your email provider (e.g., Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo).

3. Change Your Password

  • If you can still access your account, change your password immediately.
  • Create a strong and unique password (12+ characters, mix of letters, numbers, and symbols).
  • Avoid reusing passwords from other accounts. Refer to Preventive Measures for tips on creating secure passwords.

4. Enable Second-Factor Authentication (2FA)

  • Activate 2FA for your email account.
  • Use an authenticator app (e.g., Google Authenticator, Authy) instead of SMS for added security. Learn more in Preventive Measures.

5. Check for Unauthorized Activity

  • Review your sent folder and account activity to identify any unauthorized actions.
  • Check if auto-forwarding rules, recovery email, or phone numbers have been changed.
  • Revert any changes made by the hacker.

6. Notify Contacts

  • Inform your contacts that your email was hacked to prevent them from falling for phishing scams.
  • Advise them not to open suspicious emails, links, or attachments sent from your account.

7. Secure Linked Accounts

Identify accounts linked to your hacked email (e.g., social media, banking, shopping sites). Change passwords for those accounts as well.

8. Check for Data Breaches

  • Use websites like Have I Been Pwned to check if your email address was part of a data breach.
  • If it was, update passwords for other accounts that may have used the same credentials.

9. Report the Incident

  • Report the hack to your email provider (e.g., Gmail’s support or Outlook’s security team).
  • If sensitive data was compromised, report the incident to local authorities or relevant organizations (e.g., your bank).

10. Monitor for Suspicious Activity

  • Watch for unusual activity in your email or linked accounts.
  • Enable account activity notifications to get real-time alerts for suspicious logins.

11. Prevent Future Hacks

  • Regularly update passwords and enable 2FA on all accounts. Refer to Preventive Measures for details.
  • Be cautious of phishing emails and suspicious links.
  • Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities unless connected through a VPN.
